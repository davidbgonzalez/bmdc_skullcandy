Overview of solution

Executive overview

This is the submission for the Skullcandy dataset for the Big Mountain Data Competition.

  • Using bigdata and machine learning methodologies to improve and simplify the process of managing brand image with multiple target audiences
  • Using bigdata and machine learning methodologies to identify key "archetype" individuals/muses wanting to engage with your brand
  • Using cloud resources to develop a cost effective POC for data collection, processing, and real-time responsive engagement from Skullcandy to their target audiences

Skullcandy's problem statements and our proposed solutions

Problem 1: Who are the non-customers who follow us and visit the same websites as our customers?

We decided that it with the dataset available and the data we could go after to take a look at Skullcandy's twitter followers and to build a generalized profile of those individuals. From there we collected information about individuals following Skullcandy's competition. What we were able to generate is a dashboard that helps to visuzalize how Skullcandy's followers engage the compettion as well as why types types of individuals follow the competition but do not follow Skullcandy.

How are followers and non-followers similar/different?

Problem 2: Who might be interested in a buds "subscription"?

Who are the heavy users? Customer IDs with multiple purchases

Problem 3:

Can we correlate competitor sentiment with their sales (NPD Data) or with our sales?

Timeseries of customer sentiment over time with respect to each competitors product and Skullcandy's products using Amazon reviews.

How can we predict competitor sentiment (positive or negative>, react to it more quickly, and take action to minimize competitor benefit and maximize our benefit?

Work up of the Amazon reviews and high prestige reviewers

How can real-time answers and actions be taken?

Automated watching of social media looking for key signals

Automated watching of Amazon reviews looking for high prestige reviewers to weigh in, engage them preemptively, watch for newer reviewers gaining traction.

Problem 4: Is someone who recently purchased a cell phone, or gaming console, now a likely purchaser of headphones, headsets, or earbuds? and How do we identify these individuals and markets appropriately?

Work up of customers/archetypes as well as purchasers (e.g. mom and grandma)
